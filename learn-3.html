Adventure Part 3: Putting Your Adventure Online

In the last two CoderDojo sessions, you created an adventure out of web-pages.

The web-pages were written in HTML (HyperText Markup Language).

We also used CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) to make the adventure look nice. You could make the page background red or the text blue.

This session, we're going to:

  • Put your adventure on the internet, so everyone can play it :-)
  • Add some Javascript code to the adventure, to get some special effects
  • Learn more about the Javascript programming language by playing someone else's adventure online. It's really cool.

Putting Your Adventure on the Internet

So far you created your adventure on your own computer - so only you can access it.

For other people to see your adventure, it needs to be on the Internet.

This means your adventure files (the web-pages and pictures) have to be 'uploaded' to a website on the Internet.

You can then give the 'web address' of your adventure to your friends and family, and they can play your adventure!

An example adventure

There's an example adventure already online for you to look at.

Your adventure will be just like it, except with its own special web address.

Open the following address in your web browser, and have a play:
http://coderdojostirling.neocities.org/coderdojo-adventure/start.html

Putting Your Adventure Online

Let's put your own adventure online.

First, make sure you have your adventure on your computer.

Don't have it? Download a new copy from http://tiny.cc/coderdojo-adventure

Next, go to https://neocities.org (when you can create a free website):

  • Sign up: give the name you want for your website, your email address and password.
  • Choose the 'Free' option
  • Click on the red 'Edit Site' button
  • Click on the 'New Folder' button, and create one called 'coderdojo-adventure'
  • Click on the 'coderdojo-adventure' folder, on the 'Manage' link
  • Click on the 'Upload' button, and select all the adventure files from your computer (webpages and images)

If everything went to plan, your adventure is now on the Internet!

Let's find the web address of your adventure.

In the 'coderdojo-adventure' folder on the Neocities website, click on the start.html file: it should open up in a new browser window.

You should be on the start page of your adventure, and it's on the Internet!

Look at web address at the top of your browser: this is the web address of your adventure that you can share with your friends and family.

Now go show a mentor. They can put a link to your adventure on the CoderDojo Stirling webpage.

You Now Have Your Own Website!

You now have your own website on the Internet.

Its web address is something like: http://your-chosen-name.neocities.org

Your adventure is in the folder http://your-chosen-name.neocities.org/coderdojo-adventure/...

But you can create other folders with other web-pages in them.

You can create and edit new folders and web-pages by clicking the red 'New Page' and 'New Folder' buttons on the Neocities website.

Or you can create web-pages on your own computer, and upload them using the 'Upload' button - like you just did with your adventure.

Adding Special Effects to Web-pages with Javascript

All websites (like your adventure) involve three main technologies:

  • HTML (HyperText Markup Language): for writing the web-pages (content)
  • CSS (Cascading Style Sheets): for making the web-pages look nice (styling)
  • Javascript code: for making things happen on your web-pages (special effects, games, anything!)
